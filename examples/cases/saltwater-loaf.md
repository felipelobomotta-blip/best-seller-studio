# Case 8: The Saltwater Loaf

## Snapshot

- Genre: cozy mystery / literary-cozy series starter
- Language: EN
- Target length: 80k words
- Planned chapters: 24
- Pipeline: V5 production
- Public manuscript: no

## Custom Scoring Dimension

```text
Fair-Play Cluing
```

This measures whether the mystery can be solved by attentive readers using real bread vocabulary rather than arbitrary puzzle logic.

## Why It Matters

The Saltwater Loaf tests whether Book Genesis can handle a cozy mystery without flattening it into generic comfort fiction. The hook is specific: a burned-out product manager inherits an artisan bread bakery, becomes a murder suspect, and solves the case through recipe-margin clues encoded in real baking concepts.

## What The Pipeline Had To Control

- fair-play clue visibility
- cozy-market content constraints
- protagonist voice without profanity or edge-for-edge's-sake
- police competence without making the amateur sleuth passive
- bread craft as clue system, not decoration

## What It Taught The System

Genre comfort still needs mechanics. The pipeline can make a cozy premise more defensible when it turns the charm object, in this case bread, into an actual detection system.
