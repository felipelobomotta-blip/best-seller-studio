# Case 6: The Seventh Manuscript

## Snapshot

- Genre: literary thriller / dark academia
- Language: EN
- Target length: 95k words
- Planned chapters: 26
- Pipeline: portable-agent foundation and drafting
- Public manuscript: no

## Custom Scoring Dimension

The project added:

```text
Unreliable Narration Craft
```

This measured whether the narrator manipulates the reader intentionally while remaining compelling, fair, and rereadable.

## Why It Matters

The Seventh Manuscript is a meta-genre stress test. It uses dark academia while also being about dark academia. The risk was not just plot failure; the risk was pretension, derivative homage, and reader backlash from a narrator who lies too cleanly or too obviously.

## What Worked In Foundation

- clear wedge: a dark academia book about the danger of reading too carefully
- conscious unreliable narrator, not accidental self-deception
- explicit risk personas, including a hostile literary/Substack reader
- dual-timeline architecture with reread payoff
- public-domain and fair-use constraints identified early

## What The Pipeline Had To Control

- not becoming a simple homage to The Secret History
- keeping The Secret History diegetic rather than ornamental
- making the reveal a confession of motive before a confession of fact
- preserving reader affection for Iris before suspicion hardens
- preventing the seminar cast from becoming aesthetic accessories

## What It Taught The System

For high-risk literary concepts, Phase 1 and Phase 2 matter more than drafting speed. Reader-risk modeling belongs in the foundation, not after the manuscript is complete. This is one of the strongest arguments for the universal core's explicit `ASSUMPTIONS.md` and early architecture artifacts.
