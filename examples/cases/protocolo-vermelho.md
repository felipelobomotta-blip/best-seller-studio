# Case 3: Protocolo Vermelho

## Snapshot

- Genre: vigilante thriller
- Language: PT-BR
- Approximate length: 67.5k words during revision
- Planned length: 80k-90k words
- Chapters: 30
- Pipeline: V4 to V5 recalibration
- Public manuscript: no

## Observed Scores

| Metric | V4 cycle | V5 cycle 1 | V5 cycle 2 |
|--------|----------|------------|------------|
| Floor | 7.5 | 6.5 | 7.0 |
| Average | 8.29 | 7.1 | 7.4 |
| CVI-Launch | 8.8 | 7.6 | 8.0 |

## Why It Matters

This case showed that V4 could overstate readiness. The vigilante concept had a strong commercial hook, but the bottlenecks were pacing, prose/voice, and dialogue texture. The V5 recalibration lowered inflated scores and made the system confront the weaker dimensions.

## What Worked

- clear Brazilian vigilante premise
- strong market wedge
- high CVI-Launch
- action architecture with obvious commercial shelf

## What Needed Work

- some chapters stayed lean
- dialogue was too clean in unrevised chapters
- recurring lexical tic patterns remained
- pacing floor limited the book more than concept originality

## What It Taught The System

Protocolo Vermelho reinforced the floor principle. A strong commercial hook cannot compensate for weak pacing or prose. It also validated the portable core's choice to score after a hard audit rather than trusting a self-approving evaluator.
