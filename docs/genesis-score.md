# Genesis Score V3.7 -- Complete Specification

The Genesis Score is the craft quality measure at the heart of Book Genesis. V3.7 is the result of ~75 calibrations tested against 5 bestsellers with 338M+ combined copies sold: Fifty Shades of Grey (150M), The Alchemist (150M), Gone Girl (20M), Atomic Habits (15M+), and Normal People (3M).

Accuracy: 85% average against these 5 benchmarks. Worst case: 70% (The Alchemist -- parable fiction has a documented ceiling due to non-craft factors like gift-ability and translation ease).

---

## Core Principle: FLOOR, Not Average

The score is the **lowest dimension among all 7**. Not the average. Not the weighted average. The lowest.

A book with 6 dimensions at 9.0 and one at 7.0 has a Genesis Score of **7.0**.

This forces the writer to strengthen ALL dimensions. You cannot compensate weak characters with brilliant prose. You cannot hide poor pacing behind deep theme. Every dimension must clear the bar.

---

## The 7 Dimensions

### 1. Originality

**What it measures:** What this book does that no other book did.

**How to evaluate:** List 3 unique elements. If you cannot list 3, the score is <= 7.0.

**What counts:** Unique premise, unexpected approach, trope subversion, original structural choices, fresh voice in a saturated genre.

**What does not count:** Being different for the sake of different. Originality must serve the story.

### 2. Theme

**What it measures:** Depth of the central question.

**How to evaluate:** Does the theme appear in >= 80% of chapters without being explicitly declared? If yes, score >= 8.0. Does a character ever say the theme? If yes, score <= 7.0.

**4 levels of weaving:**
- MACRO: The question of the entire book
- MEDIUM: How each part reflects the question from a different angle
- MICRO: How each chapter touches the question (advancing, not repeating)
- NANO: Images, metaphors, recurring details that echo the theme

### 3. Characters

**What it measures:** Dimensionality, contradictions, transformation arc, and CHAOS.

**How to evaluate:**
- Each main character has wound + lie + arc?
- Cover the name -- are the voices distinguishable?
- Minimum 2 contradictions per main character
- V4 addition: Does the character have a chaos profile (irrelevant thoughts, cognitive distortions, unprompted memories, failed emotional management)?
- Do characters think about things that have nothing to do with the scene?
- Do secondary characters have their own chaos moments?

**7 layers:** Wound, Lie, Conscious Desire, Unconscious Need, Contradictions, Voice, Transformation Arc.

### 4. Prose and Voice

**What it measures:** Sentence-level quality + recognizable authorial personality + anti-AI compliance + voice under pressure.

**How to evaluate:**
- Open 3 random pages. Is the voice identifiable?
- Are there sentences an editor would highlight positively?
- Does it pass the anti-AI check (20 patterns)?
- Does the voice CHANGE under emotional pressure?
- Is there at least 1 stopping sentence per chapter (literary fiction)?
- Is the prose invisible (thriller)?
- Does it sound like 2am truth-telling (memoir)?

### 5. Pacing and Coherence

**What it measures:** Speed variation + internal consistency + page-turning quality + value shifts.

**How to evaluate:**
- Does the reader turn the page in >= 80% of chapters?
- Does each chapter have a value shift (positive to negative or vice versa)?
- Is there speed variation within each chapter (acceleration + deceleration)?
- Does vulnerability appear before competence? (3+ pages of competence without vulnerability = problem)
- Does each chapter end with a hook (question, shifted emotion, lingering image)?

### 6. Emotion

**What it measures:** Real emotional investment from the reader.

**How to evaluate:**
- Identify the 3 highest-impact moments. Do they work?
- Does the reader care about the character BEFORE suffering with them?
- Are multiple emotional techniques used (not just physical sensation + metaphor)?
  - Contradiction, understatement, dramatic irony, wrong reaction, accumulated mundane detail, body rebels
- Is there an emotional SURPRISE per chapter (the wrong emotion)?
- "Would You Remember This Tomorrow?" test: if the reader finished this chapter before bed, what image or line would they remember the next morning? If nothing specific, the chapter has failed.

### 7. Configurable Dimension

**What it measures:** Varies by project. Defined at the start.

**Options:**
- **Surreal/Kafkaesque** -- Bureaucratic interludes at 3 levels: ILLUSTRATE (<=7.0), COMMENT (7.5-8.0), REVEAL (>=8.5)
- **Market** (default if no device) -- Clear audience, comp titles, cultural moment, word count, CVI-Launch score
- **Worldbuilding** -- Consistent rules, sensory details, real cost for characters
- **Epistolary** -- Documents that reveal more than narrative, with their own voice
- **Humor** -- Humor that serves the scene, consistent, with register variation
- **Custom** -- Any device defined by the user

## Genre-Adjusted Profiles

V3.7 adjusts ALL 7 dimensions based on genre. What scores 8.0 in literary fiction is different from what scores 8.0 in a thriller. The 8 genre profiles are:

| Genre | Prose Priority | Pacing Priority | Character Depth | Theme Weight | Chaos Frequency |
|-------|---------------|----------------|----------------|-------------|----------------|
| Literary Fiction | Very High | Medium | Very High | High | 1+ per chapter |
| Memoir | High (authenticity > beauty) | Medium | Very High | High | 1+ per chapter |
| Thriller | Low (invisible prose) | Very High | Medium | Low | 1 per 2-3 chapters |
| Commercial Fiction | Medium | Very High | Medium | Medium | 1 per 2-3 chapters |
| Romance | Medium | High | High | Medium | 1 per 2 chapters |
| Sci-Fi/Fantasy | Medium | High | Medium | Medium-High | 1 per 2 chapters |
| Prescriptive NF | Very High (clarity) | High | N/A (no characters) | High | Skip (author) |
| NF Narrativo | High | High | Medium (subjects) | High | Apply to subjects |

**Genre-specific prose targets for 8.0+:**
- Literary Fiction: At least one STOPPING sentence per chapter
- Thriller: Prose should be INVISIBLE. If the reader notices a sentence, you broke the pace.
- Memoir: Authentic voice = 8.0+ even without literary fireworks. Reach for truth, not beauty.
- Prescriptive NF: Clarity > beauty. The best prescriptive prose makes the reader feel smarter.

## Anti-Inflation Protocol (10 Rules)

LLMs systematically inflate scores. These 10 rules prevent that:

### Rule 1: Evidence Required
Every score must cite a specific passage from the manuscript. "Prose 9.0" with no citation = invalid score.

### Rule 2: +0.5 Ceiling Per Iteration
If a dimension scored 7.5 in iteration 1, the maximum in iteration 2 is 8.0. Larger jumps require extraordinary evidence (a complete rewrite of the relevant sections).

### Rule 3: Reference Comparison for High Scores
When scoring any dimension >= 9.0: "Would a professional editor agree that this dimension is at the level of [specific published book]?" If the answer is not a confident yes, score <= 8.5.

### Rule 4: Cross-Check Adjacent Dimensions
When one dimension improves, re-verify the ones it touches. Prose went up? Check if Pacing held. Characters improved? Check if Theme is still woven.

### Rule 5: Floor Calibration
The floor mechanism itself prevents inflation. 5 dimensions at 9.0 and one at 7.0 = score 7.0.

### Rule 6: External Benchmark Anchoring (V3.1)
Specific published works define score anchors:
- Normal People (Sally Rooney): Prose 8.5-9.0, Character 9.0, Emotion 9.0, Pacing 7.5
- Gone Girl (Gillian Flynn): Prose 8.0, Character 9.0, Pacing 9.5, Originality 9.0
- Atomic Habits (James Clear): Prose 7.5 (clarity), Pacing 8.5, Theme 8.0
- The Alchemist (Coelho): Prose 7.0, Theme 9.0, Emotion 8.5 (parable ceiling)
- Fifty Shades: Prose 6.0, Pacing 8.5, Emotion 7.5, Market 9.5

When scoring any dimension, place the manuscript NEXT TO the benchmark. If the manuscript's prose is not demonstrably at the level of the reference, the score cannot match the reference's score.

### Rule 7: Scope Declaration
At the start of evaluation, declare: "This evaluation covers [scope]. It does not evaluate [what it cannot evaluate]." No evaluator should claim to assess market timing, reader psychology, or cultural moment.

### Rule 8: Discovery Test
After scoring, ask: "Did I discover anything that surprised me, or did I confirm what I expected?" If every score is expected, the evaluation may be confirmation bias. Look harder.

### Rule 9: Devoted Reader Test
For each dimension, ask: "Would the Devoted Reader of this genre -- the person who reads 50+ books/year in this genre -- rate this as above average for the genre?" The Devoted Reader is harder to impress than the general reader.

### Rule 10: Oscillation Analysis
Track scores across iterations. If a dimension oscillates (7.5 -> 8.0 -> 7.5 -> 8.0), the evaluator is uncertain. Flag for deeper analysis rather than splitting the difference.

## Anti-AI Scan (20 Patterns)

### Original 10

1. **Forced symmetry** -- "By day X, by night Y." Real prose is asymmetric.
2. **Empty poetic vocabulary** -- "Tapestry," "symphony," "dance," "journey" as metaphors.
3. **Automatic rule of three** -- Three adjectives, three examples, every time.
4. **Excessive em dashes** -- More than 2-3 per page.
5. **Empty metaphors** -- "A symphony of colors." Delete if it does not add specific meaning.
6. **Dramatic "And" openings** -- "And in that moment, everything changed."
7. **Pseudo-philosophical closings** -- Chapters ending with a neat life lesson.
8. **Excessive parallelism** -- "She did X. She did Y. She did Z."
9. **Overly smooth transitions** -- "Little did she know."
10. **Described emotions** -- "She felt a wave of sadness." Show through action and body.

### New 10 (from system analysis)

11. **The Explanatory Extension (#1 PIPELINE FINGERPRINT)** -- Every observation completed and explained. "The water is louder than expected. Not roaring, but persistent, the sound of something that doesn't care whether you're listening." CUT the second sentence. This pattern appeared in EVERY chapter of a 14-chapter test manuscript. When you write a simile, STOP. Do not extend it.
12. **Binary Negation Openers** -- "Not X. Not Y. [What it actually is]." Define by assertion, not negation.
13. **Precision Flex** -- Unnecessarily exact numbers. "247 cases" when "more cases than she could count" is more human.
14. **Emotional Control Demonstration** -- Character notices emotion, manages it, continues. Sometimes the management must FAIL.
15. **Authoritative Description** -- Settings described with encyclopedic confidence. Characters experiencing a place for the first time should notice LESS and be confused by MORE.
16. **Philosophical Asides** -- Universal truths masquerading as character thoughts. If you can put it on a coffee mug, rewrite it.
17. **Clean Dialogue** -- Characters speaking in orderly turns, each line precisely responsive. Real conversation overlaps, fragments, goes sideways.
18. **Thematic Echo Chamber** -- Every detail resonating with the theme. Allow 30-40% of details to be pure TEXTURE.
19. **Graduated Reveal** -- Establish normal, introduce anomaly, escalate, close on tension. This is ONE structure. Use others.
20. **Emotional Temperature Report** -- Periodic check-ins with the character's body at regular intervals like vital signs.

### Genre-Adjusted Thresholds

Human bestsellers score 0-13/20 on the anti-AI scan. The target is NOT zero.

| Genre | Target (Clean) | Over-corrected (Suspicious) |
|-------|---------------|---------------------------|
| Literary Fiction | 0-3 patterns | 0/20 is suspicious |
| Memoir | 0-4 patterns | 0/20 is suspicious |
| Commercial Fiction | 0-8 patterns | 0-2/20 is suspicious |
| Prescriptive NF | 0-12 patterns | 0-5/20 is suspicious |

Benchmarks from real bestsellers:
- Normal People (literary): 1/20
- Where the Crawdads Sing (commercial): 7/20
- The Midnight Library (commercial): 13/20

Some patterns are features of accessible prose, not AI fingerprints. Do not fight the genre's natural voice.

**Pattern #11 Hard Check:** After completing any evaluation, re-scan specifically for Pattern #11 (Explanatory Extension). Count instances. If more than 2 per chapter average, flag for mandatory revision regardless of other scores.

## CVI-Launch (Commercial Viability Index -- First Year)

CVI-Launch predicts first-year commercial performance. It is separate from the Genesis Score because craft quality and commercial viability are correlated but not identical.

### 6 Inputs

| Input | Weight | How to Assess |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| Market Timing | 20% | Is the genre trending? Is there cultural momentum for this topic? Are comp titles recent and selling? |
| Comp Title Freshness | 15% | Are the 3-5 comp titles from the last 3 years? Are they still selling? Has the subgenre peaked? |
| Hook Strength | 20% | Logline test: does someone say "tell me more" or "cool"? First-page test: would a stranger read page 2? |
| Platform/Audience | 15% | Does the author have an existing audience? Newsletter, social media, speaking engagements? Zero platform = score capped at 6.0. |
| Pacing Score | 15% | Directly from Genesis Score Dimension 5. Pacing is the #2 predictor of commercial success. |
| Genre Convention Compliance | 15% | Does the book meet genre reader expectations? Word count, structure, tone, content warnings if needed? |

### CVI-Launch Scale

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1.0-3.0 | Not commercially viable in current form |
| 4.0-5.0 | Niche audience only |
| 6.0-7.0 | Publishable, modest commercial potential |
| 7.0-8.0 | Strong commercial potential |
| 8.0-9.0 | High commercial potential |
| 9.0-10.0 | Breakout potential |

**Gate:** CVI-Launch >= 7.0 for editorial submission. Below 7.0, the book may be well-crafted but not market-ready.

## CVI-Legacy (Commercial Viability Index -- 20 Years)

CVI-Legacy predicts long-term cultural staying power. Some books sell modestly at launch but become classics. Some explode at launch and vanish in 3 years.

### 5 Inputs with Engagement-Adjusted Weights (V3.7)

The weights shift based on the book's primary engagement type:

| Input | Default Weight | Aspiration/Identity Override |
|-------|---------------|---------------------------|
| Re-readability | 25% | 20% |
| Emotional Resonance | 25% | 20% |
| Shareability | 20% | 15% |
| Identity Effect | 15% | 25% |
| Cultural Vocabulary | 15% | 20% |

**Re-readability:** Does the book reward a second reading? Are there details that gain new meaning after the ending? Does the reader want to return? Comfort re-reads count (not just "new discovery" re-reads).

**Emotional Resonance:** Does the book create a lasting emotional imprint? Can the reader recall specific moments months later? Does it change how they think about something?

**Shareability:** Would the reader recommend it? To whom? Through what channel (word of mouth, social media, gift)?

Shareability formula: MAX channel score x 0.6 + AVG all channels x 0.4. This does not penalize books that dominate a single channel (e.g., word of mouth only).

**Identity Effect:** Does reading this book make the reader feel something about THEMSELVES? "I'm not alone." "I'm brave for facing this." "I understand something most people don't." This is what makes readers RECOMMEND books. At least once every 3-4 chapters, include a moment that creates this effect.

**Cultural Vocabulary:** Does the book introduce terms or concepts that readers adopt into their own speech? "The Dip" (Seth Godin), "Love Language" (Gary Chapman), "Flow" (Csikszentmihalyi). Books with strong cultural vocabulary have longer commercial tails.

### CVI-Legacy Calculation

CVI-Legacy uses Genesis scores directly (no normalization). Each input maps to specific Genesis dimensions:

- Re-readability -> Originality + Theme + (Re-read architecture from foundation)
- Emotional Resonance -> Emotion + Characters
- Shareability -> Hook Strength + Emotional peaks + Quotability
- Identity Effect -> Emotion + Theme + Reader self-recognition moments
- Cultural Vocabulary -> Configurable dimension + branded terms penetration

### CVI-Legacy Scale

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1.0-3.0 | Forgotten within 2 years |
| 4.0-5.0 | Remembered by fans, not general public |
| 6.0-7.0 | Steady backlist seller |
| 7.0-8.0 | Cultural reference within genre |
| 8.0-9.0 | Cross-genre cultural impact |
| 9.0-10.0 | Generational work |

### Prescriptive NF Override

For prescriptive non-fiction, CVI-Legacy adds a 6th input:

**Framework Utility (25%):** Does the book provide a framework the reader can actually USE? Is the framework simple enough to remember and explain to someone else? Prescriptive NF with strong frameworks (Atomic Habits, 7 Habits, Getting Things Done) have dramatically longer commercial tails.

When Framework Utility is active, other weights are reduced proportionally.

## Engagement Types (5 Mechanisms)

Every book has a primary engagement mechanism that determines how the reader connects to the text. The foundation document specifies a ranked list (primary + secondary + tertiary). The Genesis Score and CVI weights adjust based on the primary type.

### 1. Empathy
Reader feels what characters feel. Prioritize intimacy, closeness, human warmth, vulnerability.
- Maximizes: Emotion, Characters
- Reader experience: "I felt that."

### 2. Fascination
Reader cannot look away. Prioritize moral complexity, ambiguity, contradiction, smart characters behaving badly.
- Maximizes: Originality, Characters
- Reader experience: "I can't believe they did that."

### 3. Self-Insertion
Reader IS the protagonist. Keep protagonist accessible, avoid excessive specificity that blocks projection.
- Maximizes: Pacing, relatable situations
- Reader experience: "That's me."

### 4. Intellectual Stimulation
Reader is learning or thinking. Prioritize world-building, systems, "aha" moments.
- Maximizes: Originality, Theme
- Reader experience: "I never thought of it that way."

### 5. Aspiration/Identity
Reader feels special. Prioritize quotable wisdom, identity-affirming moments, reader empowerment.
- Maximizes: Identity Effect, Cultural Vocabulary
- Reader experience: "I'm the kind of person who reads this."

When the primary and secondary engagement types conflict (e.g., Self-Insertion needs a blank protagonist but Empathy needs specific vulnerability), the tension point IS the book's identity. Do not resolve the conflict. Inhabit it.

## Theoretical Ceiling

No Genesis Score should ever reach 10.0. The theoretical ceiling by genre:

| Genre | Practical Ceiling | Rationale |
|-------|------------------|-----------|
| Literary Fiction | 9.5 | 10.0 reserved for genre-defining works assessed by history |
| Memoir | 9.5 | Authenticity can achieve near-perfection |
| Thriller | 9.0 | Prose ceiling is structural (invisible prose caps at 8.5) |
| Commercial Fiction | 9.0 | Genre conventions create natural ceiling |
| Prescriptive NF | 9.0 | Framework utility has diminishing returns |
| Parable Fiction | 8.5 | Non-craft factors (gift-ability, translation) dominate |

Any evaluator scoring above the genre ceiling must provide extraordinary evidence and explicit comparison with the genre's highest-rated published works.

## Evaluation Protocol for Long Manuscripts

1. **Progressive evaluation** -- Evaluate at the end of each part/act, not just the final manuscript.
2. **Deliberate sampling** -- For each dimension, select 3-5 SPECIFIC excerpts from different parts of the book.
3. **Localized evidence** -- Every score must cite chapter + excerpt. Scores without location = invalid.
4. **Internal comparison** -- Chapter 1 and chapter 10 must be at the same level. If not, the score is that of the weaker one.
5. **Comp title passage comparison** -- Place the manuscript's prose NEXT TO a passage from a published bestseller in the genre. Articulate specifically what the comp title does that the manuscript does not.
6. **Cross-book pattern detection** -- After producing a book, compare the output against all previous pipeline output. Same opening structure? Same emotional technique? Same simile architecture? Flag for revision.
